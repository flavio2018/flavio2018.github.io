# Publication kinds

<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 08/08/2021</i></p>

Taking into account the different kinds of papers I read, as well as looking at the bibliography of papers, I think I can recognize different kinds of publications in computer science.

- **technical publications**: very specific results are presented, concerining either mathematical proofs or experimental evidence about a specific algorithm or technique. I group here both theoretical and experimental work since *the scope* of the publication is limited in a very specific topic. Examples: [Deep Reinforcement Learning with Double Q-learning](@van_hasselt_deep_2015.md), [Understanding the difﬁculty of training deep feedforward neural networks](@glorot_understanding_2010.md).
- **review articles**: these are the publications taking broadly into account the last works published in a field or regarding a topic and give an (often opionated) perspective about them. Examples: [Symbolic Behaviour in Artificial Intelligence](@santoro_symbolic_2021.md), [A simple guide to chaos and complexity](@rickles_simple_2007.md).
- **perspectives**: these are commentary works usually written by leading scholars in a field in which some research is reviewed with the goal to shape the future path of research in that field. Examples: [What Learning Systems do Intelligent Agents Need? Complementary Learning Systems Theory Updated](@kumaran_what_2016.md), [Deep learning for AI](@bengio_deep_2021.md).

The different kinds of publications that exists of course mirror the dynamics of the scientific community[^community].
