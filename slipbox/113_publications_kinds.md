# Publication kinds

<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 08/08/2021</i></p>

Taking into account the different kinds of papers I read, as well as looking at the bibliography of papers, I think I can recognize different kinds of publications in computer science.

- **technical publications**: very specific results are presented, concerining either mathematical proofs or experimental evidence about a specific algorithm or technique. I group here both theoretical and experimental work since *the scope* of the publication is limited in a very specific topic. Examples: [[@van_hasselt_deep_2015|Deep Reinforcement Learning with Double Q-learning]], [[@glorot_understanding_2010|Understanding the difﬁculty of training deep feedforward neural networks]].
- **review articles**: these are the publications taking broadly into account the last works published in a field or regarding a topic and give an (often opionated) perspective about them. Examples: [[@santoro_symbolic_2021|Symbolic Behaviour in Artificial Intelligence]], [[@rickles_simple_2007|A simple guide to chaos and complexity]].
- **perspectives**: these are commentary works usually written by leading scholars in a field in which some research is reviewed with the goal to shape the future path of research in that field. Examples: [[@kumaran_what_2016|What Learning Systems do Intelligent Agents Need? Complementary Learning Systems Theory Updated]], [[@bengio_deep_2021|Deep learning for AI]].

The different kinds of publications that exists of course mirror the dynamics of the scientific community[^community].
