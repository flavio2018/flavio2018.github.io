Santoro et al. ([[@santoro_symbolic_2021]]), when listing some of the properties which a model exhibiting symbolic behaviour should show, include constructiveness. They define it as the ability to create new symbols, by assigning meaning to previously meaningless substrate.  
  
One of the reason why a cognitive agent might want to do so, is to lighten the cognitive burden on thinking something.  
  
This came to me as one of the reasons why symbolic notations of natural numbers could have come about in the first place: to create a "handy" reference to a (big) quantity to a thinker.
