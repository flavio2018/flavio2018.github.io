## Bibliometrics
- mainly citation data
- it can be useful to evaluate one's (and one's group) work, identify top journals and top researchers in a topic.
- the limitations of bibliometrics are:
	- it does not apply to all fields in the same way
	- does not take into account the work age of the researcher
	- ...
- h-index: the greatest *h* such that an author published at least *h* paper that have been cited *h* times.
- g-index: the greatest *g* such that an author published at least *g* paper that have been cited *g2* times.
- i10-index: number of papers with at least 10 citations 
- www.scimagojr.com is a tool to evaluate journals; in particular there is a quantile value that is indicative of the quality of the journal (although it has caveats, e.g. does not take into account the age of the venue)
- http://portal.core.edu.au/conf-ranks/ does the same for conferences
- on google scholar you can create alert to track citations to a specific paper
