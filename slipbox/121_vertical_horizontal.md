# Vertical and horizontal
<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 13/08/2021</i></p>

Recently, it has been recognized that students need to acquire in the course of their studies both so-called vertical skills, i.e. those related to their specific field of study (e.g. mathematical knowledge for hard scientists), and so-called horizontal skills, or soft skills, that are relative to *the way in which* we work, either by ourselves or with other people (team work, public speaking, leadership, and so on).

I find that even solely to acquire knowledge in an interesting, involved way - i.e. to have new ideas - it is necessary to do something similar. As I have already written[^gerarchie], it often happens that new ideas come from the influence of knowledge formulated in a different field of study or application from the one that the person having the idea usually works in. Furthermore, for many intellectually lively people it is natural to be curious about many different topics, facts and fields of study.

For both reasons I mentioned above, I believe that individual study proceed according to two directions of movement: assuming that personal knowledge is a tree, studying should both deepen and widen the branches (or roots) of this knowledge tree. Out of metaphor, one should focus partially on acquiring new knowledge (in the most general sense, including also techniques) in its own field of study, and partially on simply exposing oneself to new ideas, facts, narratives that one finds interesting.
