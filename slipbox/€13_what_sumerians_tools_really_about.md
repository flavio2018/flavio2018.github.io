Indeed, a man using either tokens or their negative impression on clay to count goods, would already have developed a concept of abstract exact numerosity.  
  
Furthermore, we can hypothesize (although we are not sure) that the tokens were used as a memory system, an archive, by people who were already able to count in their minds. Therefore, the counting tools used by sumerians tell us more about the first agricultural societies than about the early counters.
