# Mice Can Learn Much Faster than Previously Thought

<p style="text-align: right">

<i>

</a>
</p>

***

- mice have a hard time learning association tasks such as "if light on the right, turn right"
- a more natural, will-driven task
- mice tend to use the same rules on average to explore a complicated maze (e.g. never turn back at decision point, alternate right and left)
- grad students performed in a similar way to mice
- mice that were thirsty, and thus rewarded by drinking water drops, showed a learning behaviour that differs from the one usually observed in mice studies, which is slow and steady. They had at some point something like a sudden understanding of the task, an intuition, and consistently started to take more complex decisions that could more directly lead them to the source of water
- Title of the study: Mice in a labyrinth: Rapid learning, sudden insight, and efficient exploration.
- the study was co-funded by Google
