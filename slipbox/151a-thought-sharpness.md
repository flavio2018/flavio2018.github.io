# Thought sharpness
<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 26/10/2021</i></p>

We tend to link concepts with words. We have ideas (mental images, representations, states) for colors (red), feelings (fear), places (home), people (mum). We have words for each of these things and they make us *relive*, in some way, some past experience. We can *say* these words and make others feel what we feel.

But what about animals? They do communicate[^animal-lang], yet they don't use words. Could it be said that in some way their mental activity is someway "less sharp" than ours, to the extent to which they don't have a particular whistle or bark to say, for example, "tree"?
