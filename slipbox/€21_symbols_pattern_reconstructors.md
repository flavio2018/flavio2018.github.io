Symbols are usually something which reminds us of something else. Hence, one possible way to look at symbols in emergentist models of cognitions is as pieces of substrate which make the internal state of the model be similar to one it had in a different (related) context, namely the one in which the reference of the symbol appeared.  
  
This property appears, to some extent, in the model by Sabathiel et al. ([@sabathiel_computational_2020](@sabathiel_computational_2020.md)), in that it creates very similar internal representations for situations in which different stimuli of the same numerosity are shown to the model.
  
However, the model was not actually exposed directly to symbols.
