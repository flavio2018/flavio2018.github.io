# Socio-technical systems

This article[^munger] is a useful perspective on how the technical systems alone are not able to "change things". They are embedded in a social, economical and cultural setting which *determines* their ways of use. Technical systems should therefore more properly looked at as *socio*-technical systems, and it is the way in which they relate to the social environment they live[^live] in that can really trigger social and cultural change.

See also [[@wajcman_tirannia_2020|La tirannia del tempo: l'accelerazione della vita nel capitalismo digitale]].

[^munger]: [[@munger_facebook_2021]]
[^live]: I'm using the word live on purpose, as technical systems *mutate* in time, both as an outcome of the creative contributions made by the people who create it and use it, and as the outcome of the system execution itself.
