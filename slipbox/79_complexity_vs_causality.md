In cognitive science, there currently exist two paradigms of mathematical modeling of human cognition: the connectionist and the "structural causal" one. The first employs neural network models to study cognitive processes, while the second one uses structured probabilistic models of cognition[^bayes].

Thinking about it, the one thing that both modeling approaches have in common is that they try to *deal with randomness*. 

The structured probabilistic approach is deeply rooted in the formal study of causality[^causality], and tries to top-down[^top]  make sense of the causal relationships involved in cognitive processes using the tools of probabilistic graphical models[^pgm].

On the other hand, the connectionist or emergentist approach represent a bottom-up approach to the understanding of cognitive processes, trying to figure out what are the conditions under which such processes *emerge*[^complex].
