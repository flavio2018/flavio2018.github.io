The article is written by someone who had a PhD and a post-doc position in Astronomy, and it does not (full) apply for CS PhD.

Advice about moving from academia to industry in the Data Science field cannot possibly be very well informed, since DS is a young discipline (maybe now, 4 years after, it can be a little bit more).

Science PhD usually don't have good skills to write software collaboratively and don't know statistic and machine learning as well as statistics or CS PhDs.

What science PhD usually do have is a capacity to do conduct rigorous scientific research - and that's what should play to your favor.

Get informed about salaries using surveys and data sources ahead of an interview.

Don't expect job postings or listings for vacancies. Often, jobs are not advertised and you need to search for companies, learn who they are, what they do and how they do it.

Choose a company for which Data Science (or ML) is actually an asset that makes them competitive. This way, they will be supportive and willing to invest over talent, innovation and infrastructure.

Consider the impact you want to have on the world. More impact means more possibility to do harm, and data scientist sure help doing a lot.
