## Project-related questions

### Present your research project
- applied MANN
	- mathematical cognition
- explainable MANN

The main object of study of my research project will be **deep learning models augmented with external memories**. These models have been proposed some some years ago in Google's Deep Mind laboratories, and have proven since then to be a promising way in which deep learning models can deal with task that are similar to the ones that classic artificial intelligence models have been trying to solve for many decades. ==For example, these networks have been shown much more proficient than standard LSTM networks at question answering tasks that required to recall specific details of a situations described in the past==. However, these models have been studied relatively less with respect to more common architectures, and therefore the extent to which and the conditions under which they can be **applied to natural data sources** and go beyond toy problems is not clear yet. The first goal of my project will thus be to answer this question, and to adapt and make new model design choices which can allow the models to adapt to more realistic scenarios. ==For example, the models could be tested using textual data collected in more naturalistic scenarios, such as in real dialogues between two people, and then the network should learn to extract the information from this somewhat noisier but more realistic data source.==
Furthermore, I also intend to use these models to answer some relevant questions which are in the middle of the cognitive science and artificial intelligence fields: i.e., how does it happen that cognitive agents invent symbols and use them? In particular, I will try to investigate on the process of **learning mathematical concepts**, since they represent a very simple case of structured symbolic knowledge that also has prominent relevance in the field of developmental and educational psychology.
My project will finally have the parallel but not secondary goal to study and design ways in which the functioning of these memory augmented neural network models can be **explained to the final user**. An external memory is a tool that the artificial cognitive agent uses to produce answers to questions, and making it clear how it does so is a key necessity in order to make these models applicable in real scenarios.

### How do you plan to conduct the research?
1. design & prototyping
2. implementation & feedback
3. finalization & dissemination

I have divided the research program in three phases: the design and prototyping phase, the test and feedback phase, and the finalization and dissemination phase.
In the first phase my goal is to study the most recent literature on the topics of the project and, with the project's goals in mind, to design possible models, choose possible data sources and problems to solve. Then, I will create some prototypical versions of the models and model inspection techniques for explainability. In the second year and stage of the research I will focus on testing the models I implemented, collecting early evidence of what works and what doesn't, also regarding the model explainability techniques. I aim then to present some of my early findings at scientific conferences, to further collect feedback from the scientific community. The third and final part of the thesis will be dedicated to finalization of the model and scale-up from prototype to full-blown model. I also aim to publish my updated findings on scientific journals. Finally, I will write my doctoral thesis.

### What are the strengths of your research project?
- models not implemented much
- very relevant topic in the scientific community: a goal for the next decade
- key social and economical utility → high impact of the research and possibility to involve the general public
- interdisciplinary research (BMCS)

I believe that the main traits that distinguish my research project from others are three: I will investigate models which show promising results but have not been tested in practical scenarios very much yet: this means that there's **room for improvement**. Secondly, the goal to engage with symbolic and structured forms of knowledge, and higher-order reasoning processes is considered one of the most prominent goals in deep learning research in the **next ten years**, as several recent review articles point out. Therefore, my research project can dialogue with the most advanced current research efforts in the field.
Finally, as a consequence of the point I just made, the research project has the potential to involve also the **general public**, and contribute to improve the general understanding of scientific research processes in general, and of artificial intelligence and deep learning systems in particular. This especially true considering that one of the goals of the research will be to make the models explainable to the final user.

### Which are the problems that may arise? What are possible ways to overcome them?
- technical issues in implementation (complex models)
	- I have technical and theoretical skills and am willing to acquire more
	- prototyping: early problems detection. Then, increase complexity
- designing ways to interpret the model's functioning (complex models)
	- complex systems are difficult to explain in general
	- many efforts from the community nowadays

I am very aware that the models I will be developing and experimenting with will be very **complex models**. Indeed, there will likely be the need to make model design choices to adapt the models to more natural data sources, and this will require to understand theoretical concepts which I don't own yet as well as new algorithms and methods I don't know. However, I think that my **technical and theoretical background** will allow me to acquire the new knowledge I will need to solve the problems that will occur, and I am also motivated to acquire new knowledge and skills to complete the research project successfully. Furthermore, I believe that through a systematic design of experiments and **prototypes** to be tested at early stages of the project, it will be possible to overcome technical difficulties more easily as well as to test several design choices in advance.

Another goal of the project which will require special attention is that of explainability: it is well known that deep learning models are not chosen for their clarity or transparency - in fact they are often referred to as **black-boxes**. It will be definitely not an easy task that of designing model inspection techniques that allow the final user to understand what is going on. However, I believe there are two important things to consider: the first one is that deep neural networks - similarly as their biological counterparts - are **complex systems**, i.e. systems where many simple units interact locally, but the emerging overall behaviour is more than just the sum of the individual parts. Explaining the functioning of complex systems is a difficult task *in general*, and it is only in recent years that these topics have gained a widespread attention as they have begun to be applied to the widest range of phenomena. The second important fact is that there is **a lot of interest and focus** in the artificial intelligence community right now on this topic, and many methods have already been designed and tested (e.g. showing what part of an input image causes a certain output in a classification task). Thus, I believe that from a review of the literature and considering the particular model at hand, it will be possible to design methods to explain the functioning of models using external memories.
