# Simulated brain networks 
<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 23/10/2021</i></p>

One possible research direction: studying the emerging patterns of "connections" between neurons of a trained artificial neural network.
Something similar was done in [[@zambra_emergence_2020|Emergence of Network Motifs in Deep Neural Networks]], [[@testolin_deep_2019|Deep learning systems as complex networks]] and a general theoretical framework can be found in [[@baronchelli_networks_2013|Networks in cognitive science]].
Another kind of work that could be compared to this one is that done on brain networks such as in [[@abrate_counterfactual_2021|Counterfactual Graphs for Explainable Classification of Brain Networks]] or [[@lanciano_explainable_2020|Explainable Classification of Brain Networks via Contrast Subgraphs]].

One possible thing to be explored is the simulation of cultural neural reuse[^derrico]. Something similar can be said to be present in works exploring some additional capabilities of some trained neural networks beyond the purpose of training (e.g. [[@nasr_number_2019|Number detectors spontaneously emerge in a deep neural network designed for visual object recognition]], [[@zorzi_emergentist_2018|An emergentist perspective on the origin of number sense]]).

Notice that, from a technical point of view, post-hoc visualization of connections in trained networks is something which can probably be designed independently of the specific network, to some extent. It could possibly be useful for explanatory purposes, and could be compared to similar efforts already done with important computer vision architectures[^microscope].

[^derrico]: [[@derrico_cultural_2018|Cultural Exaptation and Cultural Neural Reuse: A Mechanism for the Emergence of Modern Culture and Behavior]]
[^microscope]: https://microscope.openai.com/
