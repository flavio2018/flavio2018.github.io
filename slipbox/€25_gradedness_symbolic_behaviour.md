To me, the most interesting feature proposed by Santoro et al. ([@santoro_symbolic_2021](@santoro_symbolic_2021.md)) is gradedness. Based on evidence from developmental psychology (and zoology → [10_coscienza_animale](10_coscienza_animale.md)), they propose that all the features of symbolic behavior could (and probably do) appear in cognitive agents at different levels of "expertise".  

I further wondered whether there could be established a hierarchy or some sort of graphical structure between such features, reflecting the order of emergence they may have in a cognitive agent.

---

[*update*] this is the tree hierarchy I came up with:

**graded**: features come at levels of expertise
↓ 
**receptive**: new symbols with new meanings can be *learned*
↓ 
**constructive**: new symbols can be *created*
↓
**embedded**: new symbols can be created *from symbols*
↓
**separable**: symbols' meanings can be separated
↓
**meaningful**: symbols can be used to describe the relationships between them
