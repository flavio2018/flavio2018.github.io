# Beyond the shape of ideas
<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 23/10/2021</i></p>

Scientific research is done using extremely specialized conceptual tools. Each field of study uses its own methods to provide evidence that supports a claim: statistical studies, mathematical proofs, computer simulations, archaeological or historical evidence, etc.

Ideas that are "intuitively" very close one to the other may therefore appear distant due to this difference in methods, which results in differences in jargon, too. One example is the study of human culture, which is done one one side using network science methods studying e.g. the diffusion of memes[^valensise], and on the other hand e.g. studying the way in which human culture and cultural innovations might have been emerged in the first place[^derrico].

The aim of the research distiller[^distill] could be considered solving this linguistic and methodological gap, bringing ideas that are essentially similar closer together in people's minds.
