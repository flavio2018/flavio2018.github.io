The work by d'Errico et al. ([@derrico_number_2018](@derrico_number_2018.md)) also introduces an interesting parallelism between cultural creation and biological evolution.  
  
The authors propose that symbols could have initially emerged as the result of "cultural exaptation".  
  
In the context of evolutionary theory, the term signifies the process of adaptation of a body part initially used for some purpose, to accomplish a different one.  
  
Cultural exaptation thus means the emergence of a new use of a pre-existing sign/item, driven by a cultural process ([@derrico_number_2018](@derrico_number_2018.md)). What ought to be modeled is exactly the cultural pressure leads to the emergence of symbols.
