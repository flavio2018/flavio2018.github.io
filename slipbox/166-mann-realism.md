# MANN realism
<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 26/10/2021</i></p>

Memory Augmented Neural Networks (MANN) are able to solve some traditional AI tasks that previously could not be solved by deep learning models.

They can do so by using an external memory that can be compared to a piece of paper to store and load past observations.

It seems to me that this is a way to skip a step in the process of dealing with more abstract forms of knowledge (compared to the way people do this).

Indeed, insights from the way in which human culture is build lead to think that it is repeated and stratified tool use that leads to the ability to process abstract knowledge[^derrico].

One possible interesting direction is further exploring the relationship that exists between the way MANN work and the hippocampus works.

[^derrico]: [[@derrico_cultural_2018|Cultural Exaptation and Cultural Neural Reuse: A Mechanism for the Emergence of Modern Culture and Behavior]]
