# Knowledge sanctuaries
<p style= "text-align: right"><i>Created 24/10/2021</i></p>

What if there existed places that materialize the importance of building scientific knowledge in the same way as churches materialize the importance of faith in God?

Thinking about this, actually these places are universities, and historical buildings of universities around the world serve exactly this purpose (thinking about the *Aula Magna* of medicine of Palazzo Bo, in Padova, or some wonderful similar places that must surely exist in Cambridge or Oxford or Sorbonne, just to name a few).

Looking at this from a contemporary perspective about the creation of knowledge as a process that serves some specific purposes in human societies, we can maybe see how churches served a similar purpose in ancient times and in the Middle Ages as universities did in modern and contemporary western societies[^post].

The kind of communicative and social processes that happen in these places has however changed a lot - and maybe the two are not even comparable.

[^post]: In [[@post_scienza_2021|La scienza non è “neutra”]] the link between churches and universities is mentioned.
