Implementing my information feeding loop using tags for horizontal and vertical topics I have found a way to manage the vast amount of resources that are available out there, without having the feeling of losing them[^collector-fallacy] and at the same time categorising them for easier later retrieval, according to to what topic is relevant to me in a specific moment.
However, time is still limited and choices still have to be made; things will still remain unread, at least in one specific moment in time. So how to choose?

# Why reading?
I have already shortly answered to this question [here](63_meta_my_knowledge_cycle).
There are at this point two axes I can categorize (and thus access) resources by: topic and kind of resource. These are important distinctions because there are different reasons to read each of these  kinds of resources. And based on these reasons (or expected outcomes) I can prioritize the reading of the different kind of resources.
The possible reasons (i.e. expected outcomes of reading) are:
1. learning ( #v books)
2. building theoretical framework ( #h books/essays and #h papers, #h web)
3. keeping updated about both technical and theoretical trends and new ideas ( #v papers, #v web)

|Papers|2|3|

Reading to learn is a slmewhat different kind of reading from the other to. It  can require more attention (e.g. if its mathematics), but I would mainly say it's different because I completely relate it to work.
Differently, reading to build theoretical framework is something I can do in my free time still having the feeling to do something interesting and pleasant by itself.
Finally, reading to keep updated is something that I relate to work in the same way, but I would say it requires less attentive reading and less dedicated time, especially when it consists in digesting web content. As such, it can probably be dedicated some small slots of time during the day, some days a week. When there's anything that requires deeper understanding it becomes learning, studying and requires longer dedicated time.

# What to read? (And when to read it?)
So, based on the distinction I did so far, we have three different kinds of reading I usually do: learning reading, curiosity/framework reading, updating reading.
The first one regards very specific technical or theoretical topics, requires allotted time during the week, and serves to answer to specific needs I have in work (e.g. learning to better structure my code or to implement MANNs). These could be called (learning) projects in GTD terms.
The second one is a to some extent a lighter kind of reading I do for pleasure, which also helps me building a wider framework I then use in my work.
The final kind of reading could be considered the natural extension to consuming the feeds of my "informational platforms" (social media, RSS feeds, channels, etc). Reading resources that come from these channels on the fly does not allow to decide what to read and causes poor use of time. Instead, allotting some dedicated time of the week to keep updated is a better strategy.

[^collector-fallacy]: https://zettelkasten.de/posts/collectors-fallacy/ One nice idea from this post is that having shorter loops of search-filing-consumption informs us better in future searches and makes us find more relevant items.
