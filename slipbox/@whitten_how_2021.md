- the model of neurons created in 1950s was based on the knowledge they had about neurons back then. Now, of course, we know more than we did and we know neurons are more complex. How much so?
- [the original work](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0896627321005018)
- > *a deep neural network requires between five and eight layers of interconnected “neurons” to represent the complexity of one single biological neuron.*
- what the authors of the study did is to train a DNN to approximate the function that regulates the decision to propagate information from dendrites to the axon in a real neurons. What the study demonstrates is that this function, usually approximated by a linear function + nonlinear activation, is much more complex.
- the specific kind neuron that was modeled was a pyramidal neuron in a rat's brain. Researchers are now convinced that the complexity of these neurons comes specifically from the dendritic trees that are in the bottom of the neuron.
- some argue that these findings should trigger changes in the design of ANN; others are not sure about the advantages
