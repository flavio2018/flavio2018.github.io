# How does the scientific community work?
The work of individual researchers or single research groups around the world, working in separate but (occasionally) communicating fields, gives as a result the global knowledge production efforts of the scientific community.

Since the work of individual research groups is (at best) locally coordinating with others in their fields of study, we can see the whole process of creation of scientific knowledge as complex process with emergent outcomes[^complexity].

The forces that influence this process can be somewhat surprising and counterintuitive: in a work from 2017[^thompson] it was shown that Wikipedia articles that are curated by experts in the field are treated as the most updated review articles on their topic. Another recent work[^mcmahan] showed that actual review articles have an important role in shaping the future scientific research on a topic, "setting the agenda".

In a recent lecture, Aaron Clause showed how the dynamics of what happens in academia can be described in terms of network science in light of the idea of prestige[^clause].
